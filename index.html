What is Online Privacy?

Online privacy, a major concern for many, refers to the ability to control what information you share online and who can access it. This includes personal details such as your name, address, phone number, and email address, as well as data like your browsing history, search queries, and social media activity. With the rise of social media and the internet, it's easier than ever for our online activity to be seen, leading to potential privacy loss, security risks, and even identity theft.

There are many laws and regulations in place to protect online privacy, but most of the laws are set for large corporations and not for individuals. This is where large corporations can still make a profit off of your personal information. Companies can track your online activity and collect data about your online behavior through their websites, apps, and other online services. With this they can sell your information to other companies, use it for targeted advertising, and gain more revenue. All through their customer's personal information.

You are entitled to your privacy rights, and it is withing your rights to exercise and protect them.

For additional information on internet laws, online privacy regulations, data collection policies, and more, visit our in-depth Information page.

Why is Online Privacy Important?

Online privacy is absolutely crucial for many reasons. Primarily, everyone is entitled to their right to privacy, and this extends to the digital world. Millions of people's information is sold to massive data brokers every day, and this is through many of the websites and apps that we use.

A large number of companies that have access to your information, subsequently selling it to others, increases the risk of data leaks. In some instances, your personal data might be compromised due to a breach in a company's security, a company you might not even be aware has your information. Just like that. Your personal data is on the dark web.

It is not just about controlling what information you share online, but it's also about protecting your personal information, your identity, and your security from potential threats in the digital world. It's a fundamental aspect of your digital life that helps maintain your integrity and safety in a rapidly evolving digital world where technological advancements nearly quadruple each year.
